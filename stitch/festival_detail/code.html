महाशिवरात्रि

Maha Shivaratri

The Great Night of Shiva. A cosmic convergence of consciousness, stillness, and the eternal dance of creation and dissolution.

The Celestial Origin

flare
Sacred Heritage • Purana Vijnana

According to the Linga Purana, Maha Shivaratri marks the night when Lord Shiva performed the Tandava—the dance of primordial creation, preservation, and destruction. It is the night when the formless Absolute (Nirguna) manifested as the Infinite Column of Light (Jyotirlinga).

format_quote
"Shiva is the silence between your thoughts; the stillness within the movement."

Devotees believe that on this auspicious night, the planetary positions are aligned in such a way that there is a natural upsurge of energy in the human system. Staying awake and maintaining an upright spine throughout the night facilitates this cosmic energy flow.

The Four Prahara Rituals

Vedic ceremonies spanning the 12 hours of the Great Night

First Prahara

6:00 PM – 9:00 PM

Panchamrut Abhishek

Offering of milk, honey, ghee, curd, and sugar. This ritual focuses on the physical body and stability.

Second Prahara

9:00 PM – 12:00 AM

Curd Abhishek

Symbolizing the purification of the mind and emotions as the night deepens into silence.

Third Prahara

12:00 AM – 3:00 AM

Ghee Abhishek

A ritual for the soul (Atman). The midnight hour represents the ultimate stillness of consciousness.
